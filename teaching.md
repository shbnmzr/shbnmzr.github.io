## Teaching Philosophy

I believe that strong pedagogical skills and strong research skills reinforce each other. Explaining a concept clearly — to a student unfamiliar with it — demands a depth of understanding that simply knowing it does not. My teaching experience has sharpened my ability to communicate complex ideas, a skill I consider essential for both academic research and cross-disciplinary collaboration.

## Experience

### Junior Tutor — Foundations of Computer Science

**Institution:** Università degli Studi di Padova  
**Course Instructor:** Dr. Adriano Luchetta  
**Duration:** October 2024 – February 2025

I served as a junior tutor for the undergraduate course *Foundations of Computer Science*, supporting students in developing foundational programming and algorithmic reasoning skills.

**Responsibilities:**

- **Led weekly programming labs**, guiding students through hands-on exercises in algorithmic problem solving and implementation.
- **Developed learning materials** including slides, annotated code samples, and worked examples to supplement lecture content.
- **Explained core CS concepts** interactively, adapting explanations to different levels of prior background.
- **Provided individualized code feedback**, helping students identify logical errors, improve code quality, and build programming confidence.

**Topics covered:** Introduction to algorithms · Data structures · Control flow and recursion · Basic complexity analysis · Programming in [language used in course]
