## In Preparation

### Aftab: Benchmarking CNN Feature Extractors in Deep Reinforcement Learning

**Authors:** Shieenavaz, Zareshahraki, Nanni  
**Supervisor:** Dr. Loris Nanni  
**Status:** In preparation  
[View Project on GitHub →](https://github.com/shbnmzr)

**Abstract (working):**  
This study investigates the effect of architectural modifications to the standard Nature DQN convolutional feature extractor on baseline performance in deep reinforcement learning tasks. We systematically benchmark a range of CNN variants across established RL environments, providing empirical evidence on which architectural choices consistently improve performance and which are task-dependent. The goal is to offer actionable guidance for practitioners designing DRL systems that rely on raw pixel input.

## Thesis

### Graph-meta: Improving Metagenomic Classification with Graph-based Learning

**Author:** Shabnam Zareshahraki  
**Advisor:** Dr. Matteo Comin  
**Institution:** Università degli Studi di Padova  
**Expected:** April 2026  

Master's thesis proposing a hybrid GNN-based architecture for taxonomic classification of metagenomic sequences. The work combines deep learning with graph-structured representations of genomic assembly graphs, outperforming state-of-the-art sequence-based classifiers on real-world multi-omic benchmarks.

## Research Interests for Future Work

My current and upcoming work touches on several open research questions I intend to pursue at the Ph.D. level:

- **Scalable GNNs for biological networks** — handling large, heterogeneous, and noisy graph structures from genomic data.
- **Generalization in metagenomic classification** — improving robustness to novel, out-of-distribution microbial taxa.
- **Graph-based multi-omic data integration** — combining genomic, transcriptomic, and metabolomic signals via unified graph representations.
- **Architecturally robust deep RL** — systematic evaluation of inductive biases in feature extraction for policy learning.
